Roof Inspection: Essential Tips to Prepare for Storm Season

While thousands flock to Arizona each year to enjoy the famously welcoming weather, storm season here is no joke. For homeowners, the stretch from June through September can bring unpredictable weather and wreak havoc on unprepared homes and gardens.

As storm approaches, it's time to ensure that your roof is ready to handle the unique challenges our desert climate can bring by doing a roof inspection. Your roof acts as an essential shield against instense heat, moonsoons, and occasional dust storms, making it crucial to take proactive steps in preparing for the upcoming season. To help you safeguard your property, we've got essential tips to help prep for storm season and get your roof inspection before it is too late.

Schedule a Professional Roof Inspection

Start by enlisting the expertise of a trusted roofing contractor, like Titan Roofing, to conduct a comprehensive roof inspection. Our experienced team will assess the overall condition of your roof, identify potential vulnerabilities, and recommend necessary repairs or maintenance. Addressing these issues in advance will minimize the risk of storm-related damage and keep headaches and repairs at bay.

Reinforce Flashing and Seals

The unique climate of Arizona, with its intense sun and occasional bursts of heavy rain, can lead to wear and tear on your roof's flashing and seals. Getting a roof inspection will ensure that these components are in tip-top condition to prevent leaks during storms. Titan Roofing's skilled professionals can reinforce flashing and seals, keeping your roof watertight and ensuring Arizona's moonsoon season is nothing to worry about.

Clean and Maintain Gutters

Well-functioning gutters are vital for effective water drainage during storms. Clear out any debris, leaves, or dirt that may clog them, as this can lead to water overflow and potential damage. Titan Roofing can assist you in gutter cleaning and maintenance, ensuring that rainwater is directed away from your home, and preventing potential water-related issues.

Check and Secure Roof Tiles/Shingles

Arizona's searing heat can cause roofing materials to expand and contract, potentially leading to loose or damaged tiles and shingles. Perform a visual roof inspection to identify any signs of damage or displacement, or enlist a pro to do so for you, if you'd rather not get up on a leader. Titan Roofing can promptly repair or replace any compromised tiles or shingles, ensuring your roof's integrity during storms.

Enhance Roof Ventilation

Proper roof ventilation is lifesaving in our intense Arizona climate, as it helps to regulate temperatures and reduce moisture buildup. Consult with the team at Titan conduct a roof inspection to evaluate your roof's ventilation system and ensure it is functioning optimally. Our experienced pros can recommend and install additional vents or fans, improving airflow and preventing potential damage from excessive heat and humidity.

Consider Impact-Resistant Roofing Materials

Living in an area prone to harrowing storms calls for special consideration when choosing roofing materials. Consider investing in impact-resistant options, specifically designed to withstand hail storms and high winds. Titan Roofing can provide guidance on durable and stylish materials that can protect your home from severe weather conditions.

Trim Trees and Remove Overhanging Branches

The desert landscape of Arizona is known for its stunning vegetation, but overhanging tree branches can pose a risk to your roof during storms. Before the season starts, trim back any branches that could potentially cause damage if they break or fall. Titan Roofing advises maintaining a safe distance between trees and your roof to minimize the chances of impact during high winds.

Prepare an Emergency Kit

Despite all your best intentions and preparations, unexpected situations can still occur. Assemble an emergency kit that includes waterproof tarps, sealants, and basic tools to address minor damage until professional help arrives. Being prepared ensures you can mitigate any immediate risks and protect your home and peace of mind until repairs can be made.
